I have had the privilege of publishing three research papers in the Materials Today: Proceedings journal by Elsevier, demonstrating my expertise in Machine Learning (ML) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) within the manufacturing domain.

The paper titled “Machine Learning Approach to Model and Predict the Shear Strength of TZM-Graphite Joint Bonded by Spark Plasma Sintering” was presented at the ICMM-2023 International Conference. Additionally, two other papers, “Prediction of Weld-Line Width and Sink-Mark Depth of Plastic Injection Moulded Parts Using Neural Networks” and “Modeling Erosive Wear of Nano-Filler Added Carbon Fiber Reinforced Polymer Composite by Artificial Neural Networks,” were presented at the ICE3MT International Conference held in October 2022. Notably, the latter received the Best Paper Award.

These research projects involved the use of advanced tools such as PyTorch, Keras, and MATLAB to model and optimize various production parameters influencing manufacturing processes. We successfully predicted critical defects and minimized associated losses, contributing significantly to enhancing process efficiency. For instance, in the award-winning paper, we utilized a 5-11-11-2 neural network architecture to predict erosive wear and weight loss in composite materials.

I collaborated closely with Professor Dr. P. Uma Maheshwera Rao from CVR College of Engineering on these projects. Together, we combined cutting-edge computational methods with domain knowledge to address challenges in manufacturing and material science, furthering innovations in predictive modeling and optimization.
